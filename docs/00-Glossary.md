# Glossary & Terminology
## Unified AI Business Assistant for CNC Factory

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2024

---

## Overview

This glossary provides definitions for key terms, acronyms, and concepts used throughout the Unified AI Business Assistant documentation.

---

## A

### Accounts Payable (AP)
Money owed by the company to suppliers/vendors for goods or services received but not yet paid for.

### Accounts Receivable (AR)
Money owed to the company by customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid for.

### Aging Report
A financial report that categorizes outstanding receivables or payables by how long they have been outstanding (e.g., current, 30 days, 60 days, 90+ days).

### API (Application Programming Interface)
A set of protocols and tools for building software applications. Allows different systems to communicate with each other.

### ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average)
A statistical model used for time series forecasting, particularly useful for demand forecasting.

---

## B

### Bill of Materials (BOM)
A comprehensive list of raw materials, components, and assemblies required to manufacture a product.

### Blue-Green Deployment
A deployment strategy where two identical production environments (blue and green) are maintained. Traffic is switched from one to the other for zero-downtime deployments.

---

## C

### Chart of Accounts
A structured list of all accounts used in an organization's accounting system, organized by account type (assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses).

### CNC (Computer Numerical Control)
Automated control of machining tools using computer programs. CNC machines are used in manufacturing for precision cutting, drilling, and shaping of materials.

### CLV (Customer Lifetime Value)
The total revenue a business can expect from a single customer account throughout their relationship.

### Credit Note
A document issued to a customer that reduces the amount owed, typically used for returns, discounts, or corrections.

---

## D

### Delivery Order (DO)
A document that confirms the delivery of goods, typically used in three-way matching with Purchase Orders and Invoices.

### Demand Forecasting
The process of estimating future customer demand for products or materials using historical data, statistical models, and machine learning.

### Docker
A platform for containerizing applications, allowing them to run consistently across different environments.

---

## E

### EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)
The electronic exchange of business documents (invoices, POs, etc.) between organizations in a standardized format.

### Elasticsearch
A distributed search and analytics engine used for full-text search, log analysis, and real-time data analysis.

### EOQ (Economic Order Quantity)
The optimal order quantity that minimizes total inventory costs (ordering costs + holding costs).

---

## F

### FIFO (First In, First Out)
An inventory valuation method where the oldest inventory items are sold/used first.

### Forecast Horizon
The time period for which a forecast is made (e.g., weekly, monthly, quarterly).

---

## G

### GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)
Standard accounting principles and procedures used in financial reporting.

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
European Union regulation governing data protection and privacy.

### GraphQL
A query language for APIs that allows clients to request exactly the data they need.

---

## I

### IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol)
A protocol for accessing email messages on a mail server, allowing multiple devices to access the same mailbox.

### Invoice
A document issued by a seller to a buyer, indicating the products, quantities, and prices for products or services provided.

### Inventory Turnover
A ratio showing how many times inventory is sold and replaced over a period.

---

## J

### JIT (Just-In-Time)
An inventory management strategy where materials are ordered and received just before they are needed in production.

### JWT (JSON Web Token)
A compact, URL-safe token format used for securely transmitting information between parties, commonly used for authentication.

---

## K

### Kubernetes (K8s)
An open-source container orchestration platform for automating deployment, scaling, and management of containerized applications.

### Knowledge Base
A centralized repository of information, documentation, and answers to frequently asked questions.

---

## L

### LIFO (Last In, First Out)
An inventory valuation method where the newest inventory items are sold/used first.

### LLM (Large Language Model)
An AI model trained on vast amounts of text data, capable of understanding and generating human-like text (e.g., GPT-4, Claude).

### LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)
A type of recurrent neural network architecture particularly effective for time series forecasting and sequence prediction.

---

## M

### MES (Manufacturing Execution System)
A system that tracks and documents the transformation of raw materials into finished goods.

### Microservices
An architectural approach where applications are built as a collection of small, independent services that communicate over well-defined APIs.

### MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
The most basic version of a product that can be released with enough features to satisfy early users and validate the concept.

---

## N

### NLP (Natural Language Processing)
A branch of AI that enables computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

---

## O

### OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
Technology that converts images of text (scanned documents, photos) into machine-readable text.

### OAuth
An authorization framework that allows applications to access user resources without exposing passwords.

---

## P

### P&L (Profit & Loss Statement)
A financial statement summarizing revenues, costs, and expenses during a specific period.

### PO (Purchase Order)
A document issued by a buyer to a seller, indicating the types, quantities, and agreed prices for products or services.

### PostgreSQL
An open-source relational database management system (RDBMS).

### Proforma Invoice
A preliminary invoice sent to a buyer before goods or services are delivered, providing an estimate of costs.

---

## Q

### Quotation (Quote)
A document provided to a potential customer outlining the price and terms for products or services before an order is placed.

---

## R

### RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)
An AI technique that combines information retrieval with text generation, allowing AI to access external knowledge bases.

### RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)
A security model that restricts system access based on user roles and permissions.

### Redis
An in-memory data structure store used as a database, cache, and message broker.

### REST (Representational State Transfer)
An architectural style for designing networked applications, commonly used for web APIs.

---

## S

### Safety Stock
Extra inventory kept on hand to protect against uncertainty in demand or supply.

### SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language)
An XML-based standard for exchanging authentication and authorization data between parties.

### SDK (Software Development Kit)
A collection of software development tools, libraries, and documentation for building applications.

### SSO (Single Sign-On)
An authentication process that allows users to access multiple applications with one set of login credentials.

---

## T

### Three-Way Matching
A process of matching three documents (Purchase Order, Delivery Order, and Invoice) to ensure accuracy before payment approval.

### TLS (Transport Layer Security)
A cryptographic protocol that provides secure communication over a network.

---

## U

### UUID (Universally Unique Identifier)
A 128-bit identifier used to uniquely identify resources in distributed systems.

---

## V

### VAT (Value Added Tax)
A consumption tax placed on a product whenever value is added at each stage of the supply chain.

---

## W

### Webhook
An HTTP callback that allows one system to notify another system when a specific event occurs.

### Workflow
A sequence of automated or manual steps that accomplish a business process.
